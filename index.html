About Me

I'm a web developer with prior experience in mathematics, music, and education. I love the orderliness of computer code and see solid logic in its progression. I recently completed a successful internship program at Riafox Inc. in Boise, Idaho, where I gained solid experience in front-end design and styling methodologies and was able to learn new platforms and languages to expand skillset. I also received training in scrum processes, content management systems, and the process of growth-driven design.

My degree is in music, my first love, while my previous professional experience is in teaching higher-level mathematics in a high-needs rural school district. It wasn't until after college I investigated programming and decided to pursue this course.

I received my programming training through the Full Stack Web Developer course at Boise CodeWorks, successfully passing their Fall 2016 cohort.

My Skills

I have experience in the following systems:

  • HTML, CSS, and Javascript
  • Responsive styling through both media queries and Twitter Bootstrap
  • Interactivity via jQuery
  • Webpage management through Node.js
  • Relational database operations with JS-Data
  • Multi-page responsive websites with AngularJS
  • Experience parsing and editing code in Python and PHP
  • Both model-view-controller and service-controller page structures
  • Unit testing through Mocha and Chai
I'm proficient in using webpage styling to duplicate any layout I’m given, but I feel my strength lies in functional programming.

My Work Philosophy

The biggest question in my mind when I am evaluating my own work is, “Would I want to use this?” After a program is working properly, I try to evaluate it through the eyes of a user. I get family and friends to look at it. I have a lot of practice at this process because of my musical training. I was constantly recording myself and playing for others to get their opinions. It was only through critically examining my own work through a lens outside myself that I was able to fine-tune and make real progress.

I subscribe to the methodology of Growth-Driven Design. What can I create first, that will have the most impact? Generally about 80% of the impact can be created by judiciously selecting 20% of the project’s features and beginning development there. I learned this lesson quickly when I was a high school trigonometry teacher with the latitude to design my own curriculum. I had to skip many of my favorite topics in order to fulfill the need to get my students college-ready. It was more important for them to understand interest rates than to explore 3-dimensional geometry. This development of curriculum gave me the skills of project triage I still use when developing applications.
