About Me

I'm a web developer with prior experience in mathematics, music, and education. I love the orderliness of computer code and see solid logic in its progression. I recently completed a successful internship program at Riafox Inc. in Boise, Idaho, where I gained solid experience in front-end design and styling methodologies and was able to learn new platforms and languages to expand skillset. I also received training in scrum processes, content management systems, and the process of growth-driven design.

My degree is in music, my first love, while my previous professional experience is in teaching higher-level mathematics in a high-needs rural school district. It wasn't until after college I investigated programming and decided to pursue this course.

I received my programming training through the Full Stack Web Developer course at Boise CodeWorks, successfully passing their Fall 2016 cohort.

My Work Philosophy

Many aspects of my life and career before becoming a programmer inform my current style and outlook. As a musician, I was encouraged to record my practice for self-evaluation, and play for family and friends to rehearse in performance settings. Today, this means that I still get family and friends to examine my work, and I do my best to evaluate my design through the eyes of a user or other observer. My biggest question is, "Would I want to use this?"

As a trigonometry teacher, I was given the latitude to design my own curriculum. I had the responsibility to help my group of seniors become college-ready before graduation. My approach involved finding and addressing holes in their knowledge and prioritizing ways to work the specific practice they needed into my daily instruction. I had to skip areas that I considered engaging, like polyhedral geometry, in order to concentrate on those I anticipated being most in need of in college and beyond, such as exponential growth and interest rates. The lesson I have taken from this experience is that necessary components must be added to a project before the flashier, bonus features.
